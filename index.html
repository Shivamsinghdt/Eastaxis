Who We Are

EastAxis Research and Advisory (EARA)

EastAxis Research and Advisory (EARA) is an independent, non-partisan think tank dedicated to advancing research, policy analysis, and strategic dialogue on Northeast India and its wider eastern neighbourhood — Southeast Asia, East Asia, and Australia.

EARA closely tracks developments in India's eastern neighbourhood policy and the implementation of the Act East Policy.

Our Vision & Mission

Our Vision

A connected eastern region

A connected eastern region defined by regional cooperation, opportunity, and shared prosperity.

Our Mission

Research, dialogue, action

Our mission is to generate rigorous research, offer strategic analysis, policy recommendations, and a platform for dialogue among governments, businesses, academic institutions, civil society organizations, and other stakeholders engaged in shaping the future of the region.

To achieve this mission, EARA seeks to cultivate a vibrant community of scholars, emerging researchers, subject experts, policymakers, and practitioners committed to addressing the region's most pressing challenges and opportunities.

Priority Axes

See our research →

EARA's research and policy engagement are organized around two priority axes. The Act East Policy constitutes a central theme across both axes, informing our analysis of regional connectivity, diplomacy, economic cooperation, security, and India's engagement with its eastern neighbourhood and the wider Indo-Pacific.

Priority Axis I

Northeast India and Its Neighbourhood

Research under this axis examines the interaction of India's Northeastern states — Assam, Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Nagaland, Mizoram, Tripura, and Sikkim — with neighbouring nations such as Bangladesh, Bhutan, China, Myanmar, and Nepal.

This axis focuses on international borders, security, connectivity, trade, migration, diplomacy, and regional cooperation, and critically assesses the implementation and impact of the Act East Policy, including connectivity initiatives, economic integration, border infrastructure, people-to-people exchanges, and subregional cooperation in the NE region.

Priority Axis II

Southeast Asia, East Asia and Australia

To complement Priority Axis I, the second axis moves beyond Northeast India and focuses on the broader regional environment that shapes India's eastern engagement — examining the political, economic, strategic, and security dynamics of Southeast Asia, East Asia, and Australia, with particular emphasis on their implications for India and the broader Indo-Pacific region.

EARA seeks to deepen understanding of emerging geopolitical and geoeconomic trends, regional institutions, and evolving patterns of cooperation and competition, with particular attention to India's engagement with ASEAN, East Asian partners, and Australia, and the opportunities and challenges arising from the implementation of the Act East Policy.
